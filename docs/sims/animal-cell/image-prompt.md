# Animal Cell Diagram — Image Generation Prompt

Please generate a new image.

## Critical Rule

**This image must contain absolutely no text, labels, arrows, callout lines, or
annotation marks of any kind.** All labeling will be added as an interactive HTML
overlay from a separate data file. Any embedded text will conflict with the overlay
system and must not appear.

---

## Image Specifications

- **Format**: PNG or high-quality JPG
- **Dimensions**: 1200 × 900 px (landscape, 4:3 ratio)
- **Background**: clean white (#FFFFFF)
- **Style**: clean scientific illustration; flat design with soft gradients;
  suitable for a high-school college placement Biology textbook

---

## What to Draw

A single eukaryotic animal cell shown in **cross-section** (as if the top half has
been sliced away to reveal the interior). The cell should be viewed from slightly
above and to the left, giving a gentle 3/4 perspective that makes the interior depth
visible without distorting the shapes.

### Cell Boundary

The outer edge of the cell is the **cell membrane**: a smooth, slightly irregular
oval shape, roughly 80% of the full image width and 75% of the image height, centered
slightly left-of-center and slightly above center in the frame. The membrane itself
is rendered as a thin double line (2–3 px each, 4 px gap between) in warm dark brown
(#5C3A1E). The interior of the double line has a very faint warm-orange tint to suggest
the lipid bilayer.

The interior of the cell is filled with **cytoplasm**: a smooth pale blue-grey
(#E8EEF5) with a very subtle radial gradient (slightly lighter at the center, slightly
darker toward the edges) to give depth. No texture or stipple — keep it smooth.

---

## The Six Structures to Draw

### 1. Nucleus

**Position**: slightly left of center, slightly above the vertical midpoint of the cell.

**Appearance**:
- A large, prominent oval shape, approximately 28% of the cell's width and 32% of
  the cell's height.
- **Nuclear envelope**: double-line ring in golden yellow-green (#7DB84A), 3 px each
  line with a 3 px gap. The envelope has 5–6 small rectangular notches evenly spaced
  around it to represent nuclear pores — these notches are dark (#333) and clearly
  visible at full resolution.
- **Nuclear interior**: filled with pale lavender (#E6E0F0) to contrast clearly
  with the cytoplasm.
- **Nucleolus**: a single dark-blue (#1A3A8F) filled circle approximately 20% of
  the nucleus diameter, positioned slightly upper-right of nucleus center.

### 2. Cell Membrane

Already drawn as the outer boundary of the cell (described above in "Cell Boundary").
The membrane should be visually prominent — it is one of the six labeled structures —
so ensure the double-line rendering is clear and the warm-orange lipid interior band
is distinguishable from the white background.

### 3. Mitochondria

**Position**: Draw **two mitochondria**:
- One in the upper-right quadrant of the cytoplasm, roughly 70% right and 30% down
  from top of the cell interior.
- One in the lower-left quadrant, roughly 25% right and 68% down.

**Appearance** (identical for both):
- Bean-shaped (elongated oval with one concave long edge), approximately 12% of
  cell width × 7% of cell height.
- **Outer membrane**: smooth rounded outline in medium teal (#2A8080), 2 px stroke.
- **Interior**: filled with pale teal (#C8E8E8).
- **Cristae**: 3–4 curved shelf-like inward folds of the inner membrane drawn as
  dark teal (#1A6060) curved lines crossing the interior of the organelle,
  clearly visible.
- The two mitochondria should be slightly different orientations (one tilted ~30°
  clockwise, the other ~20° counterclockwise) to look natural.

### 4. Cytoplasm

Already drawn as the pale blue-grey interior fill of the cell. To give it visual
identity as a distinct structure:
- Scatter approximately 15–20 very small, irregular, slightly translucent pale-grey
  blobs (2–4 px diameter) throughout the cytoplasm away from organelles to suggest
  the aqueous gel texture. These should be subtle, not distracting.
- The open area between organelles in the right-center of the cell should be
  relatively unobstructed and clearly show the pale blue-grey fill — this is the
  region the callout marker for "Cytoplasm" will point to.

### 5. Ribosomes

**Position**: Scattered in two locations:
- Approximately 25–30 tiny dots attached to the outer surface of the rough
  Endoplasmic Reticulum membrane (described below).
- Approximately 15 additional tiny dots free-floating in the cytoplasm,
  distributed throughout the cell interior but denser near the nucleus.

**Appearance**:
- Each ribosome is a tiny filled circle, 3–4 px diameter.
- Color: warm dark orange (#C05010) so they stand out clearly against both the
  pale cytoplasm and the ER membrane.
- Do not draw them as pairs or show subunit detail — simple dots is correct at
  this scale.

### 6. Endoplasmic Reticulum (Rough ER)

**Position**: A network of flattened membrane sacs wrapping around the right
and lower-right side of the nucleus, extending outward toward the right edge
of the cell interior. The ER should occupy roughly the region from the right
edge of the nucleus to about 65% of the cell's width.

**Appearance**:
- Draw 4–5 parallel, slightly curved flattened membrane sacs (cisternae) arranged
  concentrically around the right side of the nucleus — like concentric partial ovals
  that echo the nucleus shape but open outward to the right.
- Each cisterna is a pair of parallel lines 4–5 px apart, colored medium purple
  (#7B4F9E), with the interior of each sac filled with pale lavender (#EDE0F8).
- The innermost cisterna connects visually to the outer nuclear envelope to show
  continuity (draw a short bridge line connecting the two outermost ER lines to the
  nuclear envelope double lines at the 3 o'clock position of the nucleus).
- The ribosome dots (described above) are densely attached to the outer face of each
  cisterna membrane pair, giving the "rough" texture.
- The ER should look like stacked curved ribbons spiraling outward from the nucleus,
  not a chaotic tangle.

---

## Composition and Spacing

- Leave clear, unobstructed open space around each major structure so that circular
  callout markers (approximately 24 px diameter) can be placed on each structure
  without visually interfering with another structure.
- The following approximate regions should be clearly identifiable and unobscured:
  - **Nucleus center**: occupied by the nucleus itself
  - **Cell membrane**: clearly visible all around the cell perimeter, especially
    at the 2 o'clock and 7 o'clock positions (upper-right and lower-left of cell edge)
  - **Each mitochondrion**: clearly isolated from other organelles by cytoplasm
  - **Open cytoplasm**: a clear patch of pale blue-grey visible in the lower-right
    quadrant of the cell interior (approximately 72% right, 65% down within cell)
  - **Ribosome cluster**: visible as a group on the ER surface and as scattered dots
    in the upper-left cytoplasm area
  - **ER body**: clearly visible as stacked curved membranes to the right of nucleus

---

## What NOT to Draw

- No text, numbers, letters, symbols, arrows, or callout lines of any kind
- No Golgi apparatus, lysosomes, centrioles, or vacuoles (not part of this simplified
  6-structure diagram)
- No cell wall (this is an animal cell)
- No scale bar
- No border box around the image
- No shadow or drop-shadow effects on the cell outline
- No background color other than white
