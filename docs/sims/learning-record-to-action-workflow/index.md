## Overview

This workflow explains why a Learning Record Store creates value only when recorded evidence is
carried through analysis to a useful educational decision or response.

## Workflow Steps

1. **Learning Activity** - A learner performs an observable action in a learning environment.
2. **Standardized Event or Record** - The source translates the observation into a consistent event.
3. **LRS or Learning-Record Warehouse** - Infrastructure validates and retains records from one or more systems.
4. **Aggregation and Analysis** - Raw records become interpretable measures or derived evidence.
5. **Feedback or Action** - The evidence informs a learner, teacher, administrator, or course author.

## Lesson Plan

### Learning Objectives

After using this diagram, students will be able to:

- trace a learning observation from its source to an educational response;
- explain the distinct role of an LRS and an analytics layer; and
- identify why record collection alone does not improve learning.

### Activities

1. **Trace the Evidence:** Choose a quiz attempt or simulation action and explain what information
   must be preserved at each workflow step.
2. **Find the Missing Consumer:** Describe what happens when an implementation stops at the LRS
   without aggregation or a downstream action.
3. **Compare Sources:** Explain how LMS events, attendance, and simulation records could enter the
   same workflow while retaining their different contexts.

### Assessment

- Can the learner distinguish a standardized event from an analytic summary?
- Can the learner explain why the LRS is not itself the final educational outcome?
- Can the learner give a concrete example of feedback or action produced by the pipeline?
