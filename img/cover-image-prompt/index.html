    Cover Image Prompt¶

    Please generate a professional-quality cover image for this textbook. This image will be used on the textbook home page and in social media previews.

    Required specifications:

    • Format: PNG
    • Wide-landscape format
    • Size: 1200 x 630 pixels (1.91:1 aspect ratio)
    • This is the Open Graph standard for social media previews

    The image has four layers, back to front: background montage, color treatment, mascot, and title text.

    Subject & Tone¶

    Learning Record Store is an intelligent textbook about how standardized xAPI learning events become trustworthy, portable evidence: accepted through an event-driven ingestion pipeline, retained in an event store, compressed into a property graph, and used for mastery and adoption decisions. Its primary audience is district administrators, classroom teachers, and intelligent-textbook authors, with software engineers, learning-technology architects, standards specialists, and advanced students as a secondary audience. The visual tone should be modern, technical, precise, trustworthy, and warmly approachable.

    Title¶

    Render this exact title, verbatim and exactly once:

    “Learning Record Store”

    Place it in the center of the image in a bold, clean, highly legible geometric sans-serif typeface. Use cream-white lettering with a subtle deep-navy shadow or a restrained translucent scrim. Keep generous negative space behind the title. Do not add a subtitle, author name, acronym, or any other words.

    Background Montage¶

    Arrange a coherent montage of these eight concepts around the title. Render all elements in one consistent premium isometric technical-illustration style so the composition feels like a single designed scene rather than disconnected panels:

    1. xAPI statement — a compact glowing record card whose visual structure suggests actor → action → learning object, using icons rather than small text.
    2. Standards and interoperability — several distinct learning tools exchanging the same structured record through clean teal connector lines.
    3. Accept-first event ingestion — a flowing stream of small record envelopes entering a gateway, then moving through an orderly durable queue.
    4. Event store — a stable cylindrical data store receiving the complete record stream, shown as the trustworthy system of record.
    5. Compressed learning graph — many fine event traces condensing into a small, clear node-and-edge graph with orange summary nodes and teal concept nodes.
    6. Concept mastery — a tasteful classroom mastery heatmap made from colored tiles, recognizable at thumbnail size and containing no names or numbers.
    7. Learning path — a prerequisite DAG whose connected nodes progress from foundational concepts toward mastery, with one highlighted evidence trail.
    8. Three operational viewpoints — small, respectful iconographic silhouettes for a district administrator, classroom teacher, and textbook author, each viewing a different dashboard derived from the same central record flow.

    Use the real LRS data-model screenshot only as conceptual reference for the visual language of tenancy, content, compressed summary nodes, and a separate event store; do not reproduce its interface, labels, legend, or paragraphs.

    Mascot¶

    Place Rowan, the attached red-panda mascot reference, in the lower-left corner, occupying about 18–22% of the image height and never overlapping the title. Preserve Rowan’s identity: cinnamon-and-cream fur, round teal glasses, deep-teal neckerchief, compact brown cross-body learning-record satchel with a small gold connected-dots emblem, large kind brown eyes, gentle smile, and welcoming raised paw. Keep the premium hand-painted 3D storybook appearance and do not redesign the character.

    Style & Composition¶

    • Illustration style: premium isometric technical illustration with subtle 3D depth and carefully integrated storybook warmth for Rowan.
    • Color palette: deep navy and teal dominate, with cream-white typography, cinnamon-orange and warm-gold accents, and restrained violet highlights.
    • Lighting and mood: luminous, confident, calm, precise, and optimistic; soft studio light with controlled glows, not neon cyberpunk.
    • Composition: exact title centered; the record flow travels gently from the upper left through the storage and graph layers toward analytics on the right; montage elements form a loose ring around the title; Rowan anchors the lower-left; preserve generous title-safe negative space and safe margins on every edge.
    • Readability: the title must remain clear at small social-preview size.

    Avoid¶

    • No text other than the exact title “Learning Record Store”.
    • No misspelled, duplicated, distorted, or partially hidden title text.
    • No dense paragraphs, code fragments, labels, legends, fake charts, names, or unreadable interface text.
    • No generic stock-photo clichés, photorealistic people, corporate handshakes, isolated lightbulbs, classroom clip art, logos, trademarks, or watermarks.
    • Do not depict one graph vertex per raw event; the many event traces must visibly compress into a much smaller summary graph.
    • Do not place the event store inside the property graph.
    • Do not let montage elements, connector lines, or Rowan overlap the title.
    • Avoid clutter, visual noise, harsh gradients, excessive glow, and cyberpunk style.
